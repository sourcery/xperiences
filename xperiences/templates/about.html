A shared meal is one of those experiences that defines what it means to be human.
Eating together with family, friends and neighbors strengthens community, bridges cultures, and builds a robust local economy.
The promise of industrialization was to improve access to food. But this comes at a price: industry makes packaged food, whereas people make real food. Sharing makes us more efficient. By following the trail of food, from farm to table, across time and across cultures, we hope to demistify the process of good food and make it more widely available.

We saw a t-shirt for sale. It read: "home cooking is hurting the restaurant industry". We figured, heck, let's give them a run for it!

Of course, we love restaurants. But there is so much more happening the in the peoples' kitchen, as the listings on this site already attest.

We've also been blown away by this project: Feed the Movement is a grassroots effort to organize a food supply chain from our region's small farms to the kitchens for the ongoing Occupy movements.


Let us stretch out our hands and identify like minded peers. Let's support the famermers and the cooks.
